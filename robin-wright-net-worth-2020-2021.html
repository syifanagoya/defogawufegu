Early Life & Biography

Robin Wright was brought to this world on April 8, 1966. She was born in Dallas, Texas, the U.S. Her full name is Robin Gayle Wright.

She was raised in San Diego by her parents Freddie Wright and Gayle Gaston. Freddie Wright was an employee at a pharmaceutical company, and Gayle Gaston was a cosmetics saleswoman. She went to La Jolla High School for her education and for her high school, she went to Taft High School in Los Angeles. Lisa Kudrow and Ice Cube also attended the same school.

Career

Robin Wright began her career in the show business at the age of 14 when she started modelling. When she was 18, she played the role of Kelly Capwell in the soap opera Santa Barbara, which earned her several Daytime Emmy Award nominations.

She then appeared in the 1987 cult film The Princess Bride. She then appeared in Forrest Gump for which she gained critical acclaim. She received Golden Globe and Screen Actors Guild nominations for her role in the Best Supporting Actress category.

In 1996, she starred in Moll Flanders playing the lead role. She then appeared with her then-husband Sean Penn in She’s So Lovely.

From 2013, she started appearing the Netflix political drama series House of Cards on the role of Claire Underwood who is the ruthless wife of political mastermind Frank Underwood.

Following the completion of season 4, she stated that she deserved equal pay as her co-star Kevin Spacey and Netflix accepted it without any hesitation. After Kevin Spacey was accused of sexual misconduct, in 2017, Robin Wright was set as the show’s new lead. Kevin Spacey was subsequently fired from the show. She also directed ten episodes of House of Cards between 2014 and 2018. She appeared in Wonder Woman alongside Gal Gadot and Chris Pine.
