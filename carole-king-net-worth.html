Early Life and Education

Carole King was born Carol Joan Klein on February 9th, 1942, in Manhattan, New York. She was brought up in Brooklyn, where she attended James Madison High School and participated in the school’s music program extensively. She also played the piano from an early age and developed a fascination for music, eventually leading her to pursue it professionally.

Career

Carole King’s career started in the 1960s when she joined with Gerry Goffin as a lyricist and writing partner. Together they wrote some of the most beloved songs in the era of American pop music, including “The Locomotion,” “Will You Love Me Tomorrow,” “Up on the Roof,” and “Natural Woman,” among others.

King managed to establish herself as a successful solo artist in 1971 with her second album, “Tapestry.” The album, which spent more than six years on the charts, selling over 25 million copies worldwide, won four Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year, Best Female Pop Vocal Performance, Record of the Year, and Song of the Year.

King continued to produce chart-topping music over the years, with more than 100 of her compositions appearing on Billboard’s Hot 100 chart. She was inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 1987, and in 2013, she was honored with the Gershwin Prize by the Library of Congress in recognition of her invaluable contributions to popular music.

Personal Life

Carole King has been married four times and has four children. She’s known for her philanthropic activities, supporting a variety of causes, including environmentalism, animal rights, and health. King was a strong advocate of the United States Democratic Party and supported Barack Obama in his presidential campaign.

Real Estate
