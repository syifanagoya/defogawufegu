9/4 WWE Raw Preview: Monstrous Steel Cage Match, Jeff Hardy Battles For The Intercontinental Title &amp;

May 17, 2024
blog

WWE Monday Night Raw is live from the Century Link Center in Omaha tonight, on a special Labor Day edition of the show featuring a monstrous steel cage match, Jeff Hardy returning to singles competition in a big Intercontinental Championship match, and a whole lot more.

Here are the points WWE.com is hitting on in their official preview of tonight’s show:

Nearly a half ton of humanity will be locked inside the dreaded steel cage as the “Monster Among Men” looks to prove his braun heading into a huge WWE Universal Championship match against Brock Lesnar. You may recall that the last time these two men locked up inside the squared circle, the ring literally broke.

The Hardy Boyz are appearing a little more “woken” each week, even if their Broken Universe seems to be held together by scotch tape at this point. But this week, Jeff Hardy will return to singles competition to challenge The Miz for the Intercontinental Championship, a title the “Charismatic Enigma” has held on four separate occasions previously. Jeff hasn’t had a championship singles match in WWE in over eight years, since dropping the now-defunct World Heavyweight Championship to CM Punk back in 2009.

The answer is probably YES. Last week two of the biggest stars in WWE today collided in an epic contract signing that led to one of the most talked-about segments of the year. Cena spit some of his best mic work of 2017 while Reigns… was also there! After the two attempted to outshine each other in a tag team match against the good brothers Karl Anderson and Luke Gallows, who will leave Omaha with the upper hand in this budding rivalry?

In the final moments of last week’s WWE Raw, Alexa Bliss defeated Sasha Banks to recapture the Raw Women’s Championship, continuing The Boss’ frustrating hot-potato title career. Before the show faded to black, Nia Jax made an emphatic statement by dumping her former bestie Bliss on her back, raising the championship and staking her claim.

Last week the big news coming out of WWE for Evil Emma is that…she actually won a match. We’re not being snarky, that’s actually what they’re choosing to focus on. So what’s next for the superstar who helped shape NXT’s original women’s revolution?Are they ready to #GiveEmmaAChance, or is too little, too late?
